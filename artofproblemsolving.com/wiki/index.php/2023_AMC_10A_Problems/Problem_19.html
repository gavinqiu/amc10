When a point \( (x, y) \) is rotated about another point \( (r, s) \) by an angle \( \theta \), the new coordinates \( (x', y') \) are given by:


\begin{cases} x' = r + (x - r) \cos \theta - (y - s) \sin \theta \\ y' = s + (x - r) \sin \theta + (y - s) \cos \theta \end{cases}
